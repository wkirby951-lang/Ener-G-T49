## Narration Script

[Clear, efficient, encouraging]

Faster EFT in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

Faster EFT is a streamlined, inquiry-based tapping approach. It prioritizes body-sensing, curiosity, and cognitive reframing over repetitive phrases. It uses fewer tapping points per round but goes deeper.

[pause 1s]

The core loop is simple. Feel it. Tap it. Ask. Notice the shift. Reframe. Repeat.

[pause 1s]

The protocol has five steps.

Step one — tune in. Close your eyes. Think of the issue. Where do you feel it in your body? Place your hand there.

Step two — tap and acknowledge. Tap on the collarbone point and say — "I notice this sensation in my body. I'm not trying to change it. I'm just noticing."

Step three — inquire. While tapping, ask open-ended questions. What shape is this sensation? What color? What texture? What temperature? If it could speak, what would it say? What emotion is underneath? What belief is underneath that? How old is this feeling?

Step four — reframe. Once you identify the belief, introduce a new possibility. "I've held this belief for a long time. It's familiar. But familiar doesn't mean true." "What if this belief isn't the whole truth?" "If I could choose a new belief, what would it be?"

Step five — anchor. Tap your new belief or set-point word through four to eight points. Recheck the body. Repeat the loop if needed.

[pause 1s]

Faster EFT uses just four key points instead of the full nine. The collarbone point for holding the felt-sense. The side of eye for cognitive reframing. Under eye for emotional processing. And top of head for integration and anchoring.

[pause 1s]

Inquiry questions to keep handy. For sensation — where is it, what shape, color, texture, temperature, weight? For emotion — what's the primary feeling, what's underneath? For cognition — what belief is attached, how old is this belief, where did it come from? For metaphor — if this sensation had a message, what would it be? For shift — what changed, what's different now?

[pause 1s]

The set point reset is key. Your emotional set point is your nervous system's normal baseline. Signs of a high set point include always being on edge, difficulty relaxing even in safe situations, and your calm still feeling tense. To reset, identify your current set-point word, choose a new one, then tap on the collarbone while repeating the new word for 30 seconds daily for 21 days.

[pause 1s]

If nothing happens physically, try a different point or slower tapping. If the sensation moves, follow it — that's processing. If emotion comes up strongly, keep tapping and breathe — don't suppress it. If you can't find a felt sense, start with any tension you can feel, like jaw tightness.

This cheat sheet is for educational reference. Faster EFT is not a substitute for professional mental health care.

[END]
