## Narration Script

[Clear, efficient, gentle]

Havening in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

Havening — Amygdala Depotentiation Technique — uses gentle touch plus distraction to rewire traumatic memories at the neural level. It was developed by Dr. Ronald Ruden. The touch generates delta brainwaves that soothe the amygdala — your brain's alarm center.

[pause 1s]

The core protocol is simple. Activate — briefly focus on the distressing memory. Haven touch — begin gentle, repetitive stroking on your upper arms, palms, or face. Distract — while touching, engage your mind with a distracter task. Check — pause and re-rate the intensity from 0 to 10. Repeat if needed.

[pause 1s]

The three Havening touch areas. Upper arms — gentle stroking from shoulder to elbow, slow and rhythmic. Palms — slow circles with your thumbs, palm resting on your lap. Face — sweeping from forehead down cheeks, or temples to jaw. Touch should feel soothing, not ticklish or irritating.

[pause 1s]

Distracter tasks to use while touching. Visualizing — a safe peaceful place, golden light, or shrinking the memory on a screen. Counting — backward from 100 by 7s, or backward from 50 by 4s. Auditory — hum a tune, recite a poem or prayer silently. Kinesthetic — imagine warm light moving through your body. Cognitive — name animals from A to Z, do mental math. Pick tasks that require real mental focus but aren't stressful. Switch tasks every 30 to 60 seconds.

[pause 1s]

When to use Havening. For sudden anxiety spikes — activate the feeling, stroke arms for 60 seconds, distract by counting down from 50, then check. For a cringe memory that pops up — activate, stroke face, hum a song, check. Before a stressful event — pre-Haven by thinking of the event, stroking arms, and counting until the charge drops. For insomnia — lying in bed, stroke arms and visualize a peaceful scene. For anger after an argument — activate the memory, stroke arms, count backward, check.

[pause 1s]

Signs of effective Havening include a deep sigh or yawn during the session, the memory feeling farther away or less vivid, the body sensation reducing or disappearing, and next time the trigger arises, it has little to no charge.

[pause 1s]

Important safety notes. Do not use Havening while operating vehicles or machinery. Do hydrate after Havening — your brain releases metabolites during reconsolidation. Do rest if you feel tired afterward. Do seek professional support for complex trauma.

Research highlights — Havening was developed by Dr. Ronald Ruden in the 2000s, targets the amygdala directly via delta wave stimulation, and is used by trauma therapists alongside EMDR and somatic therapies. Published case studies show rapid resolution of phobias and PTSD symptoms.

This cheat sheet is for educational reference. For diagnosed PTSD or complex trauma, work with a certified Havening practitioner.

[END]
