## Narration Script

[Clear, efficient, grounding]

Deep breathing in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

Deep breathing is the fastest, most portable tool for calming your nervous system. By consciously slowing and deepening your breath, you activate the vagus nerve, increase heart rate variability, lower cortisol, and signal safety to your brain.

[pause 1s]

There are five essential techniques. Box breathing — inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4 — best for focus and calm under pressure. 4-7-8 breathing — inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8 — best for sleep and deep relaxation. Diaphragmatic breathing — belly rises on the inhale, falls on the exhale — for daily stress relief and vagus nerve activation. The three-part breath — belly, then ribs, then chest on the inhale, and reverse on the exhale — for full oxygenation. And simple awareness — observing the natural breath without control — for mindfulness and emotional regulation.

[pause 1s]

Box breathing is the quick reference technique. Imagine tracing a square. Inhale for 4 counts as you go up one side. Hold for 4 across the top. Exhale for 4 down the other side. Hold for 4 across the bottom. Repeat for 1 to 5 minutes. Use it before meetings, during stress, or anytime you need to refocus.

[pause 1s]

4-7-8 breathing is the sleep and anxiety technique. Exhale completely through your mouth. Close your mouth and inhale through your nose for 4 counts. Hold for 7 counts. Exhale through your mouth for 8 counts. Repeat 4 to 8 times. The 8-count exhale activates the vagus nerve.

[pause 1s]

Diaphragmatic breathing is the anytime technique. Place one hand on your belly and one on your chest. Inhale slowly through your nose — your belly should rise while your chest stays still. Exhale through your mouth — your belly falls. Aim for 6 to 10 breaths per minute. Do this sitting, standing, or lying down.

[pause 1s]

How to build a breathing practice. In the morning, do diaphragmatic breathing for 5 minutes. At midday, box breathing for 2 minutes before lunch or after a stressful call. In the evening, 4-7-8 breathing for 5 minutes before bed. Anytime you're stressed, 5 rounds of box breathing takes just 60 seconds.

[pause 1s]

Common challenges. If you can't hold your breath for 7 seconds, reduce the counts — try 2-3-4 or 3-4-5 instead. If you feel light-headed, return to normal breathing and don't force the depth. If you can't focus, use a guided session or a breathing app. If you fall asleep, sit upright instead of lying down. If you get bored, try a different technique each day.

[pause 1s]

The science behind the breath. Inhale — heart rate slightly increases. Exhale — heart rate slightly decreases. Slow breathing at 6 breaths per minute creates optimal heart rate variability and a calm state. Extended exhales activate the vagus nerve, triggering the relaxation response. Deep breathing lowers blood pressure, reduces cortisol, and improves digestion.

[pause 1s]

And finally, the 60-second emergency reset. Use this anywhere, anytime. Exhale completely. Inhale slowly for 4 counts. Hold for 4 counts. Exhale for 4 counts. Repeat 3 to 5 times. Notice the calm. Done — you just reset your nervous system.

Breathing practices are generally safe. If you have a respiratory or cardiovascular condition, consult your doctor before practicing extended breath holds.

[END]
