## Narration Script

[Warm, clear, informative]

Faster EFT is an evolution of classic EFT Tapping, developed by Robert G. Smith in the 2000s. While traditional EFT focuses on tapping through meridian points while repeating phrases, Faster EFT is a more streamlined, cognitive-somatic approach that emphasizes felt-sense awareness, cognitive shifts, and rapid reframing.

[pause 2s]

Here's the key difference. Classic EFT uses long setup statements and repetitive reminder phrases, tapping through all nine points. Faster EFT uses brief, direct acknowledgements, single-pass tapping with inquiry, and a focus on the felt sense in the body rather than the story about the problem.

[pause 2s]

The core method follows a simple loop.

First, identify the felt sense — where do you feel the issue in your body?

Second, tune in — bring your attention to that sensation.

Third, tap — while holding the sensation, tap on just two to four key points.

Fourth, inquire — ask yourself "what's here now?" or "what changed?"

Fifth, reframe — introduce a new perspective if needed.

And sixth, repeat — continue until the sensation shifts or dissolves.

[pause 2s]

A core idea in Faster EFT is the emotional set point — the habitual level at which your nervous system feels normal. If you've been stressed for years, your set point is high-stress. Faster EFT works to reset this set point to a state of calm, one layer at a time.

[pause 2s]

Who benefits from Faster EFT? Anyone who finds classic EFT too repetitive or rigid. People who respond well to inquiry-based approaches. Those who prefer working with body sensations over affirmations. Chronic pain sufferers. And anyone interested in performance and peak state coaching.

[pause 2s]

Sessions feel more like a conversation with yourself. You tap while checking in with your body, asking what's changed, and following the thread. This can lead to rapid insights and faster processing.

This information is for educational purposes. For diagnosed conditions, work with a licensed professional.

[END]
