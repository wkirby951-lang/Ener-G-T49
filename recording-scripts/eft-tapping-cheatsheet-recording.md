## Narration Script

[Clear, efficient, encouraging — like a coach's quick rundown]

EFT in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

Tapping on acupressure points sends calming signals to the amygdala — your brain's alarm center — reducing the stress response while you focus on a problem.

[pause 1s]

There are nine tapping points. Let me name them quickly so you remember.

The karate chop — side of your hand.

Eyebrow — start of your eyebrow by the bridge of your nose.

Side of eye — the bone at the outer corner.

Under eye — the bone below your eye.

Under nose — between your nose and upper lip.

Chin — the crease between your chin and lower lip.

Collarbone — where your collarbones meet your sternum.

Under arm — about four inches below your armpit.

And top of head — the crown.

[pause 1s]

The basic protocol has five steps.

One — identify the issue.

Two — rate its intensity from 0 to 10.

Three — setup statement. Tap the karate chop point and say three times: "Even though I have this problem, I deeply and completely accept myself."

Four — tap through the sequence. Tap each point five to seven times while repeating a reminder phrase.

Five — re-rate the intensity. Repeat rounds until it drops to 0 or 2.

[pause 1s]

When to use EFT? For anxiety attacks, tap faster and focus on the physical sensation. For phobias, tap while visualizing the feared situation from a distance. For chronic pain, tap on the sensation itself. For insomnia, tap lying down. For cravings, tap while thinking of the craved item. And for performance, tap before a meeting or exam.

[pause 1s]

Common setup statements include: "Even though I feel anxious, I accept myself anyway." Or "Even though this feels impossible, I'm open to seeing it differently."

[pause 1s]

Signs of release include a deep sigh or yawn, a sudden urge to take a deep breath, tears, feeling heat or tingling, or the issue suddenly seeming farther away.

[pause 1s]

If your number won't drop, check for a deeper layer. If you're feeling worse, that's temporary — keep tapping or take a break. If you're unsure which point to use, any point is beneficial.

[pause 1s]

There's also a three-minute emergency sequence. Tap karate chop while saying "Even though I'm overwhelmed right now, I'm safe and I'm okay." Then tap through all eight points with the phrase "this overwhelm." Take a breath. Then do a second round with "I'm choosing calm." Finish at the top of your head with "peace."

[pause 1s]

Research highlights: EFT reduces cortisol by 24 to 43 percent in a single session. A meta-analysis of 107 studies found large effect sizes for anxiety, depression, and PTSD.

This cheat sheet is for educational reference. EFT is a complementary practice and not a replacement for medical or mental health care.

[END]
