## Narration Script

[Warm, steady, inviting voice]

Welcome. Take a moment to settle wherever you are. Sit up tall — not rigid, just dignified. Let your hands rest on your thighs or in your lap.

[pause 4s]

Close your eyes if it feels right, or lower your gaze to a soft focus.

[pause 3s]

Take a full breath in through your nose… and let it out through your mouth with a sigh.

[pause 4s]

Another one — in fully… and out completely.

[pause 4s]

On the third breath, just breathe naturally and notice what it feels like to arrive in this moment. You don't have to be anywhere else right now.

[pause 5s]

Let's check in with your body quickly. Notice — are you holding tension anywhere? Jaw? Shoulders? Stomach? Just notice without trying to change it.

[pause 5s]

That awareness is the first step of breathing meditation.

[pause 2s]

Now, place one hand on your belly and one hand on your chest.

[pause 4s]

We're going to practice diaphragmatic breathing — belly breathing. When you inhale, let your belly rise like a balloon. When you exhale, let it fall.

[pause 4s]

Don't try to force it. Just invite your breath deeper.

[pause 3s]

Inhale slowly through your nose… feel your belly rise into your hand.

[pause 5s]

Exhale through your mouth… feel your belly fall.

[pause 5s]

Again — slow inhale, belly rises…

[pause 5s]

Slow exhale, belly falls…

[pause 5s]

Notice if your chest hand stays relatively still. The movement should be mostly in the belly.

[pause 4s]

Let's do three more rounds of slow, deep diaphragmatic breathing.

[pause 3s]

Inhale… fill your belly…

[pause 5s]

Exhale… completely…

[pause 6s]

Inhale… deeper this time…

[pause 5s]

Exhale… with a soft sigh…

[pause 6s]

One more — the fullest breath yet… fill from the bottom up…

[pause 5s]

And release… letting everything go…

[pause 6s]

Notice how calm your body feels when you breathe this way. This is your natural state, available whenever you return to the breath.

[pause 3s]

Now we'll shift to 4-7-8 breathing — a classic technique for calming the nervous system.

[pause 3s]

The pattern is — inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7 counts, exhale for 8 counts. I'll count for you.

[pause 3s]

Let's begin.

Inhale… 2… 3… 4.

[pause 4s]

Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7.

[pause 7s]

Slow exhale… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8.

[pause 8s]

Good. Notice the calm that comes with the extended exhale.

[pause 3s]

Round two.

Inhale… 2… 3… 4.

[pause 4s]

Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7.

[pause 7s]

Exhale… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8.

[pause 8s]

Round three — the long exhale is activating your vagus nerve — the body's brake pedal.

Inhale… 2… 3… 4.

[pause 4s]

Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7.

[pause 7s]

Exhale… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8.

[pause 8s]

Let your breath return to normal. Notice the spaciousness in your mind and body.

[pause 5s]

Now we'll practice open breath awareness — no special pattern, just watching the breath as it is.

[pause 4s]

Let your breath find its own natural rhythm. Don't control it — just observe it.

[pause 5s]

Notice the sensation of the breath at the nostrils — the coolness of the inhale, the warmth of the exhale.

[pause 8s]

Notice the pause at the top of the inhale… and the pause at the bottom of the exhale.

[pause 8s]

Your mind will wander. That's normal. Every time you notice it's wandered, simply say "thinking" gently, and come back to the breath.

[pause 8s]

Each return to the breath is like a rep at the gym — you're building your attention muscle.

[pause 8s]

Notice the rising and falling of your chest or belly. Let the breath be your anchor to the present moment.

[pause 8s]

If thoughts come, let them pass like clouds. You are the sky. The breath is the constant.

[pause 8s]

Now, slowly expand your awareness — feel the breath in your whole body. From your head to your feet.

[pause 5s]

Notice your body sitting here. The air on your skin. The sounds in the room.

[pause 5s]

Gently wiggle your fingers and toes.

[pause 4s]

Take one deeper breath — in… and out.

[pause 4s]

When you're ready, slowly open your eyes.

[pause 4s]

Take a moment to appreciate yourself. You just spent 16 minutes in conscious awareness of your breath. That's a gift you gave yourself.

[pause 3s]

You can use any of these techniques throughout your day. Box breathing before a meeting — 60 seconds. Diaphragmatic breathing during a break — 2 minutes. 4-7-8 before sleep — 1 minute. Breath awareness whenever you need to reset — anytime.

[pause 4s]

Your breath is always with you. It's your most portable tool for peace.

[END]
