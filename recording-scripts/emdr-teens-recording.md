## Narration Script

[Warm, calm, steady voice — slightly slower than normal speech]

[conversational, friendly]

Hi there. I'm glad you're here. Before we begin, take a deep breath with me.

[pause 3s]

Breathe in through your nose… and out through your mouth.

[pause 3s]

[empathetic]

This is a safe space. Nothing you feel is wrong. We're just going to let your brain do what it already knows how to do — heal.

[pause 2s]

Think of something that's been on your mind lately. Maybe a test coming up. Maybe something someone said. Maybe just… everything feeling like too much. You don't have to tell me what it is. Just picture it — like a screenshot in your mind.

[pause 5s]

[soft, guiding]

Notice where you feel it in your body. Your chest? Your stomach? Your shoulders? It's okay. Just notice.

[pause 4s]

Now rate how uncomfortable it feels, from 0 to 10. Zero means totally calm. Ten means the worst it could be. Just get a number in your head.

[pause 4s]

A negative thought that goes with this — like "I'm not good enough" or "I can't handle this" or "Everyone's judging me." Just let the words appear.

[pause 3s]

And a positive thought you'd rather believe. Something like "I am enough" or "I can get through this." It doesn't have to feel true yet. We're going to help it feel true.

[pause 3s]

[steady, rhythmic]

Okay. Let's begin.

---

[rhythmic — voice synchronizes with bilateral stimulation pace]

Bring that memory to mind now. The image. The feeling in your body. The negative thought.

[pause 3s]

Just let it be there. And follow the light with your eyes. Left… right… left… right…

[guide through silently — 20 seconds bilateral stimulation]

Good. Take a breath.

[pause 4s]

What are you noticing now? Any shift — in the image, your feelings, your body?

[pause 5s]

Just notice it. Don't try to change anything. And let's go again.

[guide through silently — 25 seconds bilateral stimulation]

[soothing]

Breathe. Whatever came up is fine. That's your brain doing its work.

[pause 3s]

Let's go back to the original memory. Is it the same? Different? Farther away?

[pause 5s]

And follow the light…

[guide through silently — 25 seconds bilateral stimulation]

[encouraging]

You're doing great. This takes courage. Take another breath.

[pause 5s]

Let's continue. Let your mind go wherever it needs to.

[guide through silently — 30 seconds bilateral stimulation]

Notice what's there now. It might be changing. That's okay. That's good.

[guide through silently — 20 seconds bilateral stimulation]

[calm, closing]

One more round. Stay with it.

[guide through silently — 20 seconds bilateral stimulation]

---

[slowing down]

Take a deep breath in… and out.

[pause 3s]

[gentle, affirming]

Now bring up that positive thought we identified earlier. Say it to yourself.

[pause 4s]

As you hold that thought, follow the light again…

[guide through silently — 15 seconds slow bilateral stimulation]

On a scale from 0 to 10, how true does that positive thought feel now?

[pause 4s]

Let's strengthen it. Keep repeating the positive thought…

[guide through silently — 15 seconds bilateral stimulation]

---

[grounding, warm]

We're going to wrap up now. Take a nice, deep breath.

[pause 3s]

Bring your hands to your chest or your belly. Feel your own warmth.

[pause 3s]

[empathetic, sincere]

You just did something really brave. You faced something difficult, and you stayed with it. That matters.

[pause 3s]

When you're ready, gently open your eyes if they were closed. Look around the room. Name three things you can see.

[pause 5s]

Two things you can hear.

[pause 3s]

One thing you can feel — the ground under you, the air on your skin.

[pause 3s]

[grounding]

You're back here, in this room, safe and okay. Well done.

[pause 2s]

If anything came up that feels too big, you can talk to a trusted adult — a parent, a counselor, a teacher. You don't have to carry it alone.

[END]
