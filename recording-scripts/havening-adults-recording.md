## Narration Script

[Deeply warm, unhurried, safe voice — creates a container of safety]

Welcome. Take a moment to arrive fully. Sit in a way that allows you to feel both supported and present.

[pause 5s]

Take three slow breaths together. Let each exhale be a little longer, a little more complete.

[pause 8s]

Set an intention for this session. Not a demand — just a direction. Something like — "I'm ready to release the charge from a memory that no longer serves me," or "I choose to feel safe in my body again."

[pause 8s]

Know that you are in control here. You can pause at any time. You can open your eyes. You can stop. Nothing will happen to you — we're simply updating how a memory is stored.

[pause 5s]

Bring to mind a specific memory that still carries emotional charge for you. This could be from last year or from decades ago. A moment of loss, fear, shame, anger, or deep sadness.

[pause 10s]

Don't tell yourself the whole story. Just let the most potent image or feeling arise.

[pause 8s]

Notice where the charge lives in your body. Be specific — is it in your chest? Your throat? Your gut? Your shoulders?

[pause 6s]

Notice the quality of the sensation — is it heavy? Sharp? Dull? Cold? Hot? Tight?

[pause 6s]

Rate the intensity from 0 to 10.

[pause 5s]

Now, before we begin the Havening, I want you to place one hand on the area of your body where you feel the charge. Just acknowledge it. Say silently — "I feel you. You've been here for a long time. I want to help you release."

[pause 6s]

Begin stroking your upper arms. From shoulder to elbow, slow and gentle. Let your touch be kind and reassuring.

[pause 6s]

This touch generates delta brainwaves — the same brainwaves of deep, healing sleep. Combined with the memory activation and distraction, this will rewire the neural pathway.

[pause 4s]

Keep stroking. Take yourself to a peaceful place in your imagination — a beach, a forest, a mountain, a garden. Use all your senses.

What do you see? The colors, the light, the movement.

[pause 6s]

What do you hear? The sounds — wind, water, birds, silence.

[pause 6s]

What do you feel? The temperature, the ground beneath you, the air on your skin.

[pause 6s]

What do you smell? Salt air, pine, earth, flowers.

[pause 5s]

Now switch to stroking your palms — slow circles with your thumbs.

[pause 4s]

Count backward from 100 by 7s — 100… 93… 86… 79… 72… 65…

[pause 8s]

Now count backward from 50 by 4s — 50… 46… 42… 38… 34… 30…

[pause 8s]

Now multiply 7 times 8, 8 times 9, 9 times 7, 6 times 8 — just let the answers come.

[pause 6s]

Switch to stroking your face — gently sweep across your forehead, down your cheeks, along your jaw.

[pause 5s]

Imagine a golden light at the top of your head. See it slowly moving down through your body, warming and soothing every cell it touches.

[pause 8s]

Now hum a melody — any song that brings you comfort.

[pause 5s]

Recite something you know by heart — a poem, a prayer, a mantra, the lyrics of a song.

[pause 6s]

Let your hands rest for a moment. Bring the original memory back to mind. What do you notice?

[pause 8s]

Is the image different? The feeling in your body? The charge level?

[pause 6s]

Begin stroking your arms again.

[pause 3s]

Picture the memory as if it's on a screen in front of you. Now imagine the screen slowly fading, becoming transparent, dissolving.

[pause 8s]

Imagine writing the memory on a piece of paper, then watching the ink fade until the paper is blank.

[pause 8s]

Now imagine a warm, healing light — white or golden — washing through the space where the memory used to be, filling it with peace.

[pause 8s]

Switch to stroking your palms or face.

[pause 4s]

Let your hands rest completely. Take a long, slow breath.

[pause 6s]

Bring the original memory to mind one final time. Notice it fully.

[pause 8s]

Rate it from 0 to 10 now.

[pause 5s]

Compare this number to where you started. This difference represents real, measurable change in how your brain stores this memory.

[pause 5s]

The memory may still be there as a fact — but the emotional charge has been depotentiated. Your amygdala no longer sees it as a threat. It's now just information.

[pause 6s]

If the number is still above 2, that's okay. There may be multiple layers or aspects to this memory. You can return to Haven each layer separately.

[pause 4s]

Place both hands on your thighs. Take a deep breath.

[pause 5s]

Name three things you can see in the room.

[pause 5s]

Three sounds you can hear.

[pause 5s]

Move your body gently — roll your shoulders, stretch your neck, wiggle your fingers and toes.

[pause 5s]

Now place one hand on your heart. Say silently — "I am safe now. That memory is in the past. I am here, in the present."

[pause 6s]

In the hours and days ahead, you may notice the memory seems fuzzy or far away, your body feels lighter or looser, you have more energy — your system isn't spending energy suppressing the charge — or you may feel a sense of unfamiliar peace.

[pause 6s]

Your brain will continue to integrate this shift over the next 24 to 48 hours. Be gentle with yourself. Rest if you need to. Hydrate well.

[pause 5s]

Take one final breath together. In… hold… and out.

[pause 6s]

When you're ready, gently open your eyes. Carry the peace you've cultivated here with you.

[pause 4s]

You've done profound work. Thank yourself. Thank your body. Thank your brain for being willing to heal.

[END]
