## Narration Script

[Clear, efficient, authoritative]

TFT in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

Thought Field Therapy is the original tapping modality, founded by Dr. Roger Callahan. It uses specific algorithms — sequences of tapping points — designed to rebalance the body's energy system.

[pause 1s]

The standard anxiety algorithm is: karate chop setup, then under eye, under arm, collarbone, then the 9-Gamut procedure, then repeat the three points.

[pause 1s]

The 9-Gamut is done by tapping the Gamut point — the back of your hand between the ring and pinky knuckles — continuously while doing nine actions. Close your eyes. Open your eyes. Eyes down and to the right. Eyes down and to the left. Roll your eyes clockwise. Roll them counterclockwise. Hum a tune. Count to 5. Hum again.

The purpose is to alternate between brain hemispheres and brain functions, helping the energy shift integrate.

[pause 1s]

Different algorithms exist for different issues. For trauma, the sequence is karate chop, eyebrow, under eye, under arm, collarbone, 9-Gamut, then repeat the points. For anger, use side of eye instead of eyebrow. For grief, add under nose and chin. For fear or phobia, start with eyebrow, side of eye, under eye, collarbone.

[pause 1s]

Psychological reversal is a block in the energy system that prevents healing. Signs include your number not dropping after two algorithm rounds, feeling more resistant to the idea of healing, or thinking "this won't work." To clear it, tap the karate chop point and say three times — "Even though some part of me doesn't want to let this go, I deeply and completely accept myself." Then re-run the algorithm.

[pause 1s]

Collarbone breathing is a TFT technique to check the energy system. Tap the collarbone point continuously, take a full breath and hold, ask yourself "Is there anything else?" while holding, then exhale. If a new issue arises, address it.

[pause 1s]

Tapping tips — use two to three fingertips, firm but not painful. Tap 5 to 7 times per point, except the Gamut where you tap continuously. Breathe between rounds. Stay focused on the issue while tapping. Re-rate after each algorithm round.

[pause 1s]

Run the algorithm daily for chronic anxiety, as needed for acute stress, before triggering events, or at bedtime for racing thoughts.

Research notes — TFT was developed by Dr. Roger Callahan in the 1980s, with 60 to 90 percent success rate for phobias in original clinical reports. Controlled studies show significant reduction in phobic anxiety.

This cheat sheet is for educational reference. TFT is not a substitute for professional medical or mental health care.

[END]
