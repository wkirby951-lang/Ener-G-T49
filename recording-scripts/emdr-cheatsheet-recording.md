## Narration Script

[Warm, clear, efficient — like a doctor giving a quick summary]

EMDR in three minutes. Here's what you need to know.

[pause 1s]

EMDR uses bilateral stimulation — eye movements, taps, or tones — to help your brain reprocess distressing memories. It's like how your body heals a physical wound, but for your mind.

[pause 2s]

The eight phases of EMDR are: history taking, preparation, assessment, desensitization, installation, body scan, closure, and re-evaluation.

The core phase is desensitization — where you hold a memory in mind while following bilateral stimulation. Your brain naturally processes and integrates the memory.

[pause 1s]

Key terms to know:

Subjective Units of Disturbance — or SUD — is your self-rated distress from zero, neutral, to ten, the worst.

Validity of Cognition — or VOC — measures how true a positive belief feels, from one, completely false, to seven, completely true.

A target memory is the specific experience being reprocessed.

And a channel is a new association that comes up during processing — always trust where your mind goes.

[pause 1s]

There are three types of bilateral stimulation: visual — following a moving dot; auditory — alternating tones through headphones; and tactile — alternating taps on your knees or hands. Standard pace is about one cycle per second.

[pause 1s]

Do not do EMDR if you're in acute crisis, actively suicidal, experiencing psychosis, under the influence of substances, or in the middle of a panic attack. Ground yourself first, then process.

[pause 1s]

For self-administered grounding, use the five-four-three-two-one technique — name five things you see, four you hear, three you feel, two you smell, one you taste. Or the butterfly hug — cross your arms and tap alternating hands on your shoulders.

The four-seven-eight breath — inhale four seconds, hold seven, exhale eight seconds — also works well.

[pause 1s]

The World Health Organization recognizes EMDR as evidence-based for PTSD. Over sixty controlled studies support its efficacy. And fMRI evidence shows EMDR reduces amygdala reactivity.

[pause 1s]

For self-guided app use, two to three times per week is recommended, with one target per session. For professional therapy, typically six to twelve sessions for a single trauma.

This cheat sheet is for educational reference. For diagnosed trauma, seek a licensed EMDR clinician.

[END]
