# Deep Breathing Meditation Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## Two-Minute Overview

Deep breathing is the fastest, most portable tool for calming your nervous system. By consciously slowing and deepening your breath, you activate the vagus nerve, increase heart rate variability (HRV), lower cortisol, and signal safety to your brain.

---

### 5 Essential Breathing Techniques

| Technique | Pattern | Best For |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Box Breathing** | In 4 → Hold 4 → Out 4 → Hold 4 | Focus, calm under pressure, pre-meeting |
| **4-7-8 Breathing** | In 4 → Hold 7 → Out 8 | Sleep, deep relaxation, anxiety attacks |
| **Diaphragmatic (Belly)** | Belly rises on in, falls on out | Daily stress relief, vagus nerve activation |
| **Three-Part Breath** | Belly → Ribs → Chest on in; Chest → Ribs → Belly on out | Deep relaxation, full oxygenation |
| **Simple Awareness** | Observe natural breath without control | Mindfulness, meditation, emotional regulation |

---

### Quick Reference: Box Breathing

```
   INHALE (4)
      ↑
HOLD (4) ← → HOLD (4)
      ↓
   EXHALE (4)
```

**When:** Before meetings, during stress, to refocus  
**Duration:** 1–5 minutes  
**Pro tip:** Imagine tracing a square with your eyes as you breathe each side.

---

### Quick Reference: 4-7-8 Breathing

1. Exhale completely through your mouth
2. Close your mouth, inhale through nose for 4 counts
3. Hold breath for 7 counts
4. Exhale through mouth for 8 counts (pursed lips)
5. Repeat 4–8 times

**When:** Bedtime, panic, anxiety spike  
**Why:** The 8-count exhale activates the vagus nerve

---

### Quick Reference: Diaphragmatic Breathing

1. Place one hand on belly, one on chest
2. Inhale slowly through nose — belly rises (chest stays still)
3. Exhale through mouth or nose — belly falls
4. Aim for 6–10 breaths per minute

**When:** Anytime, anywhere — can be done sitting, standing, or lying down

---

### How to Build a Breathing Practice

| Time of Day | Suggested Practice | Duration |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Morning** | Diaphragmatic breathing on waking | 5 min |
| **Midday** | Box breathing before lunch or after a stressful call | 2 min |
| **Evening** | 4-7-8 breathing before bed | 5 min |
| **Anytime stressed** | 5 rounds of box breathing | 60 sec |
| **During meditation** | Breath awareness | 10–20 min |

---

### Common Challenges & Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Can't hold breath for 7 seconds | Reduce counts: try 2-3-4 or 3-4-5 |
| Feel light-headed | Return to normal breathing; don't force depth |
| Can't focus on breath | Use a counting app or guided session |
| Fall asleep during practice | Sit upright instead of lying down |
| Get bored | Try a different technique each day |

---

### The Science of Breath

- **Inhale** → heart rate slightly increases (sympathetic activation)
- **Exhale** → heart rate slightly decreases (parasympathetic activation)
- **Slow breathing (6 breaths/min)** → optimal HRV, calm state
- **Extended exhale** → vagus nerve activation → relaxation response
- **Deep breathing** → lowers blood pressure, reduces cortisol, improves digestion

---

### 60-Second Emergency Reset

*Use this anywhere, anytime:*

1. Exhale completely
2. Inhale slowly for 4 counts
3. Hold for 4 counts
4. Exhale for 4 counts
5. Repeat 3–5 times
6. Notice the calm

**Done.** You just reset your nervous system.

---

*Breathing practices are generally safe for everyone. If you have a respiratory or cardiovascular condition, consult your doctor before practicing extended breath holds.*
