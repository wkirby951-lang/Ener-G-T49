# Deep Breathing Meditation — Adults (37–65+)

**Duration:** 25 minutes  
**Age Group:** Adults (37–65+)  
**Focus:** Chronic stress relief, grief support, deep relaxation, sleep preparation, emotional release  
**Technique:** Full Yogic Breath (Dirga Pranayama) + 4-7-8 + Extended Breath Awareness

---

## Script

### Phase 1: Deep Arrival (0:00–4:00)

*(Deep, unhurried, resonant voice — every word carries weight)*

"Welcome. I invite you to arrive completely — leaving behind the tasks, the worries, the shoulds. This is your time."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Find a comfortable seated or reclining position. If you're sitting, let your spine be tall but not rigid. If you're lying down, let your body be fully supported."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Close your eyes gently. Let your hands rest wherever they're comfortable — on your lap, your thighs, or by your sides."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Take three long, slow, complete breaths. Each one deeper than the last."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"First breath — inhale through your nose… and exhale through your mouth, releasing the surface tension of the day."

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"Second breath — deeper now. Fill your belly, your ribs, your chest… and exhale completely, letting go of what you've been carrying."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Third breath — the fullest yet. Inhale everything you need… hold for a moment… and exhale everything you're ready to release."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"On your next breath, breathe naturally and simply notice: how does it feel to be here, right now, with nothing to do but breathe?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

---

### Phase 2: Three-Part Breath — Dirga Pranayama (4:00–10:00)

"Now we'll practice a three-part breathing technique from the yogic tradition. It's called Dirga Pranayama — the Complete Breath."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"We'll breathe into three parts of your torso: first the belly, then the ribs, then the upper chest."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Place one hand on your belly and one hand on your chest."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

**Part 1 — Belly:**

"Inhale slowly into your belly. Feel it rise like a gentle wave under your hand."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Exhale — feel the belly fall."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Repeat — inhale, belly rises… exhale, belly falls."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

**Part 2 — Ribs:**

"In your next inhale, fill the belly first, then let the breath expand into your ribs — feel your rib cage widen sideways."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Exhale — ribs contract, belly softens."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Again — inhale into belly, then ribs… exhale fully."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

**Part 3 — Full Breath:**

"Now, on your inhale, fill the belly, then the ribs, then let the breath rise into your upper chest — a complete, three-part inhale."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Exhale — release from the chest, then the ribs, then the belly. Empty completely."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Let's do three full rounds of the Complete Breath."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Breathing in — belly… ribs… chest…"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Breathing out — chest… ribs… belly…"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Inhale — three parts, one smooth wave…"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Exhale — releasing fully…"

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"One more — your deepest yet. Inhale into belly… ribs… chest… filling to capacity…"

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"Exhale — chest… ribs… belly… empty…"

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Notice the stillness after a complete exhale. Rest there for a moment."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

---

### Phase 3: 4-7-8 Breathing — Extended (10:00–14:00)

"Now we'll move into 4-7-8 breathing — a powerful technique for deep relaxation and sleep preparation."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"The ratio is: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. The long exhale activates the vagus nerve, signaling safety to your entire nervous system."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Let's begin with three rounds."

"Round 1:"

"Inhale… 2… 3… 4."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7."

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"Exhale slowly… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Round 2 — deeper now:"

"Inhale… 2… 3… 4."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7."

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"Exhale… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Round 3:"

"Inhale… 2… 3… 4."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hold… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7."

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"Exhale… 2… 3… 4… 5… 6… 7… 8."

*(Pause 10 seconds)*

"Let your breath return to its natural rhythm. Notice the deep quiet in your body. This is your parasympathetic nervous system — the rest-and-digest state — fully engaged."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

---

### Phase 4: Extended Breath Awareness (14:00–20:00)

"Now we'll practice open, non-judgmental awareness of the breath — no pattern to follow, just being present with each inhale and exhale."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Let your breath be natural. Don't control it. Simply witness it."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Notice where you feel the breath most clearly — at the nostrils, in the rise and fall of your chest, or in your belly."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Notice the quality of each breath — is it long or short? Deep or shallow? Smooth or uneven?"

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Notice the pauses — the brief stillness at the top of the inhale and the bottom of the exhale."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Your mind will wander. This is what minds do. Each time you notice it's wandered, gently bring your attention back to the breath — without judgment, without frustration."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Each return to the breath is a moment of mindfulness. You're strengthening your ability to be present."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Imagine your breath as a gentle tide — coming in, going out. You are the shore, steady and still, watching the tide."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"If emotions arise — sadness, grief, joy, peace — let them be there. Let them move through you like weather. You are the sky. The breath is the constant."

*(Pause 10 seconds)*

"Rest here for a few more moments, in the simple experience of being alive and breathing."

*(Pause 15 seconds)*

---

### Phase 5: Body Scan & Integration (20:00–23:00)

"Now, expand your awareness from the breath to your whole body."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Slowly scan your body from head to toe, noticing any sensations."

"Crown of your head — any tingling, warmth, or stillness?"

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Your face — your forehead, eyes, cheeks, jaw. Is there softness here?"

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your neck and throat — open and relaxed."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Your shoulders and chest — your heart space. Breathe into this area."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your belly — soft and full. Your breath rising and falling here freely."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your hips and lower back — releasing any deeply held tension."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your legs and feet — grounded, supported, heavy with relaxation."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your entire body is breathing. Every cell is receiving oxygen. Every cell is releasing what it no longer needs."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

---

### Phase 6: Closing & Carry-Forward (23:00–25:00)

"Now, slowly bring your awareness back to the room. Notice the sounds around you. The air on your skin. The surface beneath you."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Gently move your fingers and toes. Roll your wrists and ankles."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Take one final, full, conscious breath together. Inhale deeply… hold… and exhale completely."

*(Pause 7 seconds)*

"When you're ready, gently open your eyes."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Take a moment before you move. Notice the quality of your mind — clearer? Quieter? More spacious?"

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"This is the gift of conscious breathing. You can return to this state anytime, anywhere, simply by bringing your attention to your breath."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Consider integrating one of these practices into your daily routine:"

- **3 minutes of 4-7-8 breathing** before bed
- **5 minutes of diaphragmatic breathing** upon waking
- **1 minute of box breathing** before any stressful event
- **10 minutes of breath awareness** during a lunch break

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Your breath is a lifelong companion and your most reliable tool for peace. Use it freely."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Thank you for this practice. May you carry this peace with you."

---

**End of session.**
