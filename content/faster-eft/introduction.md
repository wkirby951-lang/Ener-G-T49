# Faster EFT — Emotional Freedom Technique (Accelerated Model)

## What Is Faster EFT?

Faster EFT is an evolution of classic EFT Tapping developed by Robert G. Smith in the 2000s. While traditional EFT focuses on tapping through meridian points while repeating phrases, Faster EFT is a more streamlined, cognitive-somatic approach that emphasizes **felt-sense awareness**, **cognitive shifts**, and **rapid reframing**.

### Key Differences from Classic EFT

| Classic EFT | Faster EFT |
|-------------|------------|
| Long setup statements | Brief, direct acknowledgements |
| Repetitive reminder phrases | Single-pass tapping + inquiry |
| Focus on the problem | Focus on the **felt sense** in the body |
| Tapping through all 9 points | Uses fewer points, more targeted |
| Often feels like a "recipe" | More conversational, exploratory |

### The Core Method

Faster EFT follows a simple iterative loop:

1. **Identify the felt sense** — Where do you feel the issue in your body?
2. **Tune in** — Bring your attention to that sensation
3. **Tap** — While holding the sensation, tap on just 2–4 key points
4. **Inquire** — Ask: "What's here now?" or "What changed?"
5. **Reframe** — Introduce a new perspective if needed
6. **Repeat** — Continue until the sensation shifts or dissolves

### The "Set Point" Concept

A core idea in Faster EFT is the **emotional set point** — the habitual level at which your nervous system feels "normal." If you've been stressed for years, your set point is high-stress. Faster EFT works to reset this set point to a state of calm, one layer at a time.

### Research Support

- **Smith (2010–2020):** Clinical reports show significant reductions in PTSD, anxiety, phobias, and chronic pain — often in fewer sessions than classic EFT
- **Feinstein (2022):** Case series demonstrated Faster EFT's effectiveness for first responders with PTSD
- Faster EFT is an emerging modality; research base is growing but smaller than classic EFT's

### Who Benefits

- Anyone who finds classic EFT too repetitive or rigid
- People who respond well to inquiry-based approaches
- Those who prefer working with body sensations over affirmations
- Chronic pain sufferers
- Performance and peak state coaching

### What to Expect

Sessions feel more like a conversation with yourself. You tap while checking in with your body, asking what's changed, and following the thread. This can lead to rapid insights and faster processing for some people.
