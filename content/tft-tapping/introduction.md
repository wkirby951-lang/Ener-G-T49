# TFT Tapping — Thought Field Therapy

## What Is TFT Tapping?

Thought Field Therapy (TFT) is the original "tapping" modality, developed by Dr. Roger Callahan in the 1980s. Dr. Callahan discovered that tapping on specific acupuncture meridian endpoints while focusing on a distressing thought could eliminate anxiety — sometimes instantly. TFT is the foundation upon which EFT and Faster EFT were later built.

### How It Works

TFT is based on the concept of **thought fields** — the specific bio-energetic patterns created by disturbing thoughts. According to TFT theory, emotional distress is caused by a disruption in the body's energy system (similar to acupuncture's meridian system). Tapping on specific points in a prescribed sequence — called an **algorithm** — rebalances the energy system and removes the distress.

### The Diagnostic Approach

A key distinction of TFT is its **diagnostic** approach. While EFT uses a one-size-fits-all sequence, TFT can involve:
- **Voice Technology (VT):** The practitioner asks specific questions and listens to subtle changes in the client's voice to determine which meridian points are involved
- **Algorithms:** Pre-tested sequences of points for specific problems (anxiety, anger, trauma, phobias, etc.)
- **Collarbone breathing:** A technique to "check" and "repair" the energy system

### The Basic TFT Algorithm (Anxiety)

The standard TFT algorithm for general anxiety follows this sequence:

1. **Karate Chop (KC):** "Even though I have this anxiety, I accept myself"
2. **Under Eye (UE):** Tap 5 times
3. **Under Arm (UA):** Tap 5 times
4. **Collarbone (CB):** Tap 5 times
5. **Gamut Point (G):** Tap continuously while performing 9 actions:
   - Close eyes → Open eyes → Eyes down and to the right
   - Eyes down and to the left → Roll eyes clockwise
   - Roll eyes counterclockwise → Hum a few notes
   - Count to 5 → Hum again
6. **Repeat steps 2–4**
7. **Re-rate intensity**

### The 9-Gamut Procedure

This is a unique TFT technique involving continuous tapping on the Gamut Point (back of hand between the ring and pinky knuckles) while performing 9 brain-stimulating actions. It's designed to "balance" both brain hemispheres.

### Research Support

- **Callahan & Trubo (2001):** Clinical reports of rapid phobia elimination — sometimes in a single session
- **Diepold & Goldstein (2000):** Published case studies showing effectiveness for trauma
- **Sakai et al. (2001):** Study showed TFT reduced anxiety in phobic participants compared to controls
- TFT remains less researched than EFT but has a dedicated practitioner base

### Who Benefits

- People who want a structured, algorithmic approach (less free-form than EFT)
- Those who respond well to specific "recipes" for specific problems
- Anxiety, phobias, anger, grief, trauma, obsessive thoughts

### What to Expect

TFT sessions are more structured than EFT. You follow a precise tapping sequence based on the algorithm for your specific issue. Some find the 9-Gamut procedure unusual, but many report powerful shifts.
