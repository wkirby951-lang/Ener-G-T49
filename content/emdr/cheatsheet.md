# EMDR Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## Three-Minute Overview of Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing

---

### What It Is
EMDR uses **bilateral stimulation** (eye movements, taps, or tones) to help the brain reprocess traumatic or distressing memories — like how your body heals a physical wound.

---

### The 8-Phase Protocol

| Phase | What Happens | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **1. History** | Identify target memories and resources | First session |
| **2. Preparation** | Build trust, teach grounding tools | 1–2 sessions |
| **3. Assessment** | Rate disturbance (0–10), identify negative belief + desired positive belief | Per target |
| **4. Desensitization** | Bilateral stimulation while focusing on the memory — the core reprocessing | Variable |
| **5. Installation** | Strengthen the positive belief with bilateral stimulation | 5–10 min |
| **6. Body Scan** | Check for residual physical tension | 3–5 min |
| **7. Closure** | Grounding and return to equilibrium | 5–10 min |
| **8. Re-evaluation** | Check progress next session | Per session |

---

### Key Terms

- **Bilateral Stimulation (BLS):** Alternating left-right sensory input (visual, auditory, or tactile)
- **SUD (Subjective Units of Disturbance):** Self-rated distress from 0 (neutral) to 10 (worst)
- **VoC (Validity of Cognition):** How true a positive belief feels, from 1 (completely false) to 7 (completely true)
- **Target Memory:** The specific experience being reprocessed
- **Negative Cognition (NC):** The negative self-belief tied to the memory
- **Positive Cognition (PC):** The adaptive belief you want to install
- **Channeling:** Free association that occurs during processing — trust it

---

### Bilateral Stimulation Options

| Type | How To | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Visual** | Follow a moving dot/light left to right | Most users; works well on screen |
| **Auditory** | Alternating tones left→right via headphones | Users who prefer to close eyes |
| **Tactile** | Alternating taps on left/right knees or hands | Users who find visual/auditory distracting |

**Standard pace:** 1–2 cycles per second (approximately 1 full left-right per second)

---

### When NOT to Do EMDR

- Currently in acute crisis or actively suicidal
- Currently experiencing psychosis or hallucinations
- Under the influence of alcohol or non-prescribed substances
- In the middle of a panic attack (ground first, then process)
- Severe dissociative disorders (work with a professional)

---

### Self-Administered Grounding Techniques

Use these if processing feels too intense:

| Technique | How To |
|-----------|--------|
| **5-4-3-2-1** | Name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you feel, 2 you smell, 1 you taste |
| **Butterfly Hug** | Cross arms, tap alternating hands on shoulders — combines grounding with BLS |
| **4-7-8 Breathing** | Inhale 4 sec → hold 7 sec → exhale 8 sec |
| **Container Visualization** | Imagine putting the memory in a sealed container for later processing |

### Research At a Glance

- **WHO** recognizes EMDR as evidence-based for PTSD (2013)
- **APA** gives EMDR a "conditional recommendation" for PTSD treatment
- **60+ controlled studies** support EMDR efficacy since 1989
- **fMRI evidence** shows EMDR reduces amygdala reactivity and strengthens prefrontal regulation

---

### Session Frequency

- **Self-guided app use:** 2–3 times per week, one target per session
- **Professional therapy:** Typically 6–12 sessions for a single trauma; complex trauma may require more
