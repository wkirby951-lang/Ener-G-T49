# EMDR Guided Session — Young Adults (19–36)

**Duration:** 18 minutes  
**Age Group:** Young Adults (19–36)  
**Focus:** Career stress, relationship anxiety, burnout  
**Goal:** Process a specific distressing memory or recurring stressor, reduce SUD (Subjective Units of Disturbance)

---

## Preparation Notes (for the app)

- Ensure user is in a quiet, comfortable space where they won't be interrupted
- Headphones recommended for audio-based bilateral stimulation
- Have water nearby
- User should know they can pause at any time if they need a break
- If user has a history of complex trauma, a full session with a licensed EMDR therapist is recommended

---

## Script

### Phase 1: History & Setup (0:00–3:00)

*(Calm, grounded voice — warm but professional)*

"Welcome. Thank you for showing up for yourself today."

*(Pause 2 seconds)*

"Before we begin, take a moment to settle in. Adjust your posture so you feel both alert and relaxed. Feet flat on the floor. Hands resting comfortably."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Take a deep breath in through your nose… hold it for a moment… and let it out slowly through your mouth."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"One more. Breathe in… and out."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Today, we're going to work with a specific memory or stressor. It could be something that happened recently — a difficult conversation, a work deadline that overwhelmed you — or something older that still feels fresh."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Let that memory come to mind now. Just the general sense of it. You don't need to go into every detail."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"What image represents the worst part of that memory? It might be a picture in your mind — a face, a location, a moment."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Now, what negative belief about yourself does this memory reinforce? Something like 'I'm not capable,' 'I'm unlovable,' 'I'm failing,' 'I'm not safe.' Just let the words come."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Notice what emotions you feel — maybe anger, fear, shame, sadness. And notice where you feel it in your body — chest tightness, knot in your stomach, tension in your jaw."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"On a scale from 0 to 10, how disturbing does this feel right now — where 0 is neutral and 10 is the worst imaginable?"

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hold that number."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"And finally, what would you rather believe about yourself? A positive cognition — something like 'I am capable,' 'I am worthy of love,' 'I am doing my best,' 'I am safe now.' It doesn't have to feel true yet. That's why we're here."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Okay. Let's begin the processing."

---

### Phase 2: Desensitization (3:00–14:00)

*(Bilateral stimulation begins — left-right eye movements at about 1 cycle/second)*

"Bring the image to mind. The negative belief. The feelings in your body. Just let it all be there… and follow the light."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 30 seconds)*

"Good. Take a breath."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"What do you notice now? Any shift? Any new sensation, memory, emotion, or thought? Just notice and describe it to yourself."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Whatever came up — that's your brain processing. Stay with it. Let's go again."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 35 seconds)*

"Breathe."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Notice what's there now. Is the image changing? Fading? Getting clearer? Moving farther away? Closer? Is there a new feeling? A memory you haven't thought of in a while?"

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Whatever it is, that's exactly what needs to come up. Don't judge it. Don't push it away. Just let it be there, and follow the light."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 40 seconds)*

"Deep breath in… and out."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Go back to the original memory again. What's different now? Is the disturbance level the same, higher, or lower?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Let's continue. Wherever your mind goes is fine. Trust the process."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 45 seconds)*

"Notice. Breathe."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"You may notice your mind wandering to other memories or thoughts. That's completely normal. Just let the bilateral stimulation keep doing its work. You don't have to try to control anything."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 40 seconds)*

"Take a breath. Check in with your body. Any tension releasing? Any area that feels different?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Let's keep going."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 35 seconds)*

"Breathe. You're doing the work. Your brain knows how to heal — we're just supporting it."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 40 seconds)*

"Take another breath. Let's check your disturbance level now — 0 to 10. Has it changed?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"One more round. Stay present with whatever is there."

*(Bilateral stimulation — 35 seconds)*

---

### Phase 3: Installation (14:00–16:00)

"Take a slow, deep breath."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Now bring up that positive cognition again. The one you want to believe. Say it to yourself, quietly."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"As you hold that thought, follow the light…"

*(Slower bilateral stimulation — 30 seconds)*

"On a scale from 0 to 10, how true does that positive belief feel right now?"

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Let's strengthen it. Keep repeating that positive thought to yourself…"

*(Bilateral stimulation — 30 seconds)*

"How about now — how true does it feel?"

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"If it's not at a 7 or higher, that's okay. We'll keep going. What would need to shift for it to feel more true?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Hold that shift in mind… and follow the light…"

*(Bilateral stimulation — 30 seconds)*

"Good. That positive belief is being installed, like a new pathway in your brain."

---

### Phase 4: Body Scan & Closure (16:00–18:00)

"Take a full, deep breath. Let your shoulders drop."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Scan your body from head to toe. Notice any remaining tension. Any tightness in your jaw, your neck, your chest, your stomach."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"If you notice any area of discomfort, bring your breath there. Imagine breathing into that space, softening it."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Now name three things you can see in the room right now."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Three things you can hear."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Move your fingers and toes. Feel yourself fully present in this room."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"You did something courageous today. Processing takes energy, and you showed up for it. Be kind to yourself for the rest of the day. Drink water. Rest if you need to."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"If any strong feelings came up that feel overwhelming, please reach out to a mental health professional or a trusted support person. You don't have to process alone."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Thank you for trusting yourself and this process. Take another breath. And when you're ready, gently open your eyes."

---

**End of session.**

*Note: This guided session is a self-administered resource for mild-to-moderate stress and is not a replacement for professional EMDR therapy. If you have a history of trauma or PTSD, work with a licensed EMDR therapist.*
