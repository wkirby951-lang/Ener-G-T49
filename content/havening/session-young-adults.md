# Havening Technique Guided Session — Young Adults (19–36)

**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Age Group:** Young Adults (19–36)  
**Focus:** Workplace stress, rejection sensitivity, intrusive thoughts, performance anxiety  
**Goal:** Depotentiate a specific stressful memory or recurring trigger using full Havening protocol

---

## Script

### Phase 1: Preparation & Activation (0:00–3:00)

*(Calm, grounded, clinical-but-warm voice)*

"Welcome. Find a comfortable seated position. Let your hands rest on your thighs, palms facing up — open and receptive."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Take a slow breath in through your nose… and out through your mouth."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Today, we're going to work with a specific trigger — something that, when you think about it, your body has a reaction. A moment that made you feel embarrassed, rejected, angry, or afraid."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Let the memory surface. You don't need to go into every detail — just enough to feel the charge."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Notice where you feel it in your body. Chest tightness? Stomach knot? Jaw tension? Heat in your face?"

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Rate its intensity from 0 to 10 — your Subjective Units of Disturbance."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hold that feeling. We're going to use it."

---

### Phase 2: Self-Havening Protocol (3:00–10:00)

"Begin by stroking your upper arms — from your shoulders down to your elbows. Slow, gentle, rhythmic. Like you're comforting a friend."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Keep stroking. Now, while you stroke, I'll guide you through a series of distractions. This combination — soothing touch plus mental engagement — is what depotentiates the memory."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

**Distraction Round 1:**

"Close your eyes. Imagine a safe, peaceful place — somewhere you feel completely at ease."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Now count backward from 300 by 7s, silently: 300… 293… 286… 279…"

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Notice your arms — keep the gentle stroking. Switch to stroking your palms — slow circles with your thumbs."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Hum a tune you know well — any song — for a few seconds."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Now imagine a bright, warm golden light filling the room, wrapping around you."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

**Check-in:**

"Let your hands rest for a moment. Check in with the original memory. Is it still as charged? Notice any change — even a small one."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

**Distraction Round 2:**

"Begin stroking your arms again."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Picture the memory as if it's on a movie screen, and you're in the audience watching it — not in it."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Now imagine the screen shrinking, getting smaller and smaller, until it's just a dot."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Switch to stroking your palms — slow, deliberate circles."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Recite a poem, a quote, or a prayer you know, silently."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Now stroke your face — gently sweep your palms across your forehead, down your cheeks."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Recall the memory one more time. What do you notice?"

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

**Distraction Round 3:**

"Keep stroking your arms, palms, or face — whichever feels best."

*(Pause 3 seconds)*

"Imagine a beautiful color — your favorite — and let it fill your entire body from head to toe."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Count to 20 in your head — but skip every number that has a 3 in it."

*(Pause 8 seconds)*

"Hum your favorite song again, just a few notes."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Picture the original memory now. Notice what's different. The image may be there, but the charge — the body feeling — is probably much less."

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

---

### Phase 3: Reconsolidation Check (10:00–12:00)

"Let your hands rest. Take a full, deep breath."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Bring the original trigger to mind one more time. Rate it from 0 to 10 now."

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"Whatever number you get — compare it to your starting number. The drop is the measure of depotentiation."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"If it's not at 0 or 1, that's okay. There may be layers. You can repeat this process for each layer."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"What's important is: the memory is now stored differently in your brain. The neural pathway has been updated. Next time this trigger comes up, it will have less — or no — charge."

---

### Phase 4: Integration & Grounding (12:00–15:00)

"Place your hands on your thighs. Take another slow breath."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Scan your body. Notice the difference from when we started. Where was the tension? Is it released now?"

*(Pause 6 seconds)*

"Name three things you can see in the room."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Three things you can hear."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Move your fingers and toes gently."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Here's what may happen over the next few days:"

- The memory will feel distant or fuzzy
- You'll realize you haven't thought about it
- Someone might bring it up and it won't bother you
- You may feel an unfamiliar sense of peace around it

*(Pause 5 seconds)*

"This is the Havening integration process. Your amygdala has been soothed. The memory is now just information — not a threat."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"You can self-Haven anytime you notice a trigger. Just:"
1. Activate the memory briefly
2. Stroke your arms/palms/face
3. Distract yourself (count, hum, visualize)
4. Check — the charge is gone

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"Take one more breath together. In… out."

*(Pause 4 seconds)*

"When you're ready, open your eyes. You just updated your brain."

---

**End of session.**

*Havening is a gentle but powerful technique. For complex trauma or PTSD, work with a certified Havening practitioner.*
