# Havening Technique Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## Two-Minute Overview

Havening (Amygdala Depotentiation Technique) uses **gentle touch + distraction** to rewire traumatic memories at the neural level. Developed by Dr. Ronald Ruden, it generates delta brainwaves that soothe the amygdala — the brain's alarm center.

---

### The Core Protocol

```
ACTIVATE → HAVEN TOUCH + DISTRACT → CHECK → REPEAT
```

**Step 1: Activate**
Briefly focus on the distressing memory or trigger. Notice the body sensation. Rate 0–10.

**Step 2: Haven Touch**
Begin gentle, repetitive stroking on one of these three areas:
- **Upper arms** — shoulder to elbow, slow and rhythmic
- **Palms** — slow circles with your thumbs
- **Face** — sweeping strokes across forehead, cheeks, jaw

**Step 3: Distract**
While touching, engage your mind with a distracter task (see below). This prevents re-traumatization while the memory is reconsolidated.

**Step 4: Check**
Pause and re-rate the intensity (0–10). The number should drop. Repeat if needed.

---

### Distracter Tasks (Use Any)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Visualizing** | Safe peaceful place, golden light, shrinking the memory on a screen |
| **Counting** | Backward from 100 by 7s, backward from 50 by 4s, skip-count by 3s |
| **Auditory** | Hum a tune, recite a poem/prayer/song lyrics mentally |
| **Kinesthetic** | Imagine the sensation of warm light moving through your body |
| **Cognitive** | Name animals A–Z, think of words that start with a letter, do mental math |
| **Sensory** | Imagine a peaceful scene with all 5 senses in detail |

**Rules of thumb:** Pick tasks that require real mental focus but aren't stressful. Switch tasks every 30–60 seconds. Keep the Havening touch continuous.

---

### Havening Touch Guide

| Area | Technique | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Upper arms** | Gentle stroking from shoulder to elbow, alternating sides | 30–90 sec per round |
| **Palms** | Slow circular motion with thumbs, palm resting on lap | 20–60 sec per round |
| **Face** | Sweeping from forehead down cheeks, or temples to jaw | 20–40 sec per round |

**Tips:**
- Touch should feel soothing, not ticklish or irritating
- You can switch areas between rounds
- Self-Havening works; being Havened by someone else can be deeper

---

### When to Use Havening

| Trigger | Micro-session (5 min) |
|---------|----------------------|
| Sudden anxiety spike | Activate → stroke arms for 60 sec → distract (count down from 50) → check |
| Cringe memory pops up | Activate → stroke face → hum song → check |
| Before a stressful event | Pre-Haven: think of the event + stroke arms + count → charge drops |
| Insomnia from racing thoughts | Lying in bed, stroke arms + visualize peaceful scene |
| Anger after an argument | Activate the memory → stroke arms + count backward → check |

---

### Signs of Effective Havening

- A deep sigh or yawn during the session
- The memory feels "farther away" or less vivid
- The body sensation literally reduces or disappears
- You feel momentarily confused about why you were upset
- The next time the trigger arises, it has little to no charge

---

### Important Safety Notes

- **Do NOT** use Havening while operating vehicles or machinery
- **Do** hydrate after Havening (your brain releases metabolites during reconsolidation)
- **Do** rest if you feel tired afterward
- **Do** seek professional support for complex trauma
- Havening is gentle but powerful — respect the process

---

### Research Highlights

- Developed by Ronald Ruden, MD, PhD (2000s)
- Targets the amygdala directly via delta wave stimulation
- Used by trauma therapists alongside EMDR and somatic therapies
- Published case studies show rapid resolution of phobias and PTSD symptoms
- fMRI evidence supports reduced amygdala reactivity post-Havening
