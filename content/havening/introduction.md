# Havening Technique

## What Is Havening?

Havening (also called Amygdala Depotentiation Technique or ADT) is a psychosensory therapy developed by Dr. Ronald Ruden in the 2000s. It uses gentle, soothing touch — specifically stroking the arms, face, and palms — combined with directed attention and distraction to **depotentiate** (or "unlock") traumatic memories at the neurological level.

### How It Works

Havening targets the amygdala — the brain's threat-detection center. When a traumatic or distressing memory is recalled, the amygdala activates a stress response. Havening uses **delta wave stimulation** via gentle touch to generate calming delta brainwaves that override the amygdala's alarm signal.

**The three core elements of Havening:**

1. **Havening Touch** — Gentle, repetitive stroking of the upper arms, palms, and face
2. **Active Attention** — Focusing on the distressing memory or sensation (activating the amygdala)
3. **Distraction** — While touching, the person is guided through a distraction technique (humming, counting, eye movements) that prevents conscious re-traumatization

The combination creates a window where the memory can be reconsolidated without the emotional charge.

### The Havening Touch

| Area | How To |
|------|--------|
| **Upper Arms** | Gentle stroking from shoulder to elbow, like comforting a child |
| **Palms** | Slow circular strokes on the palms |
| **Face** | Gentle sweeping strokes across the forehead, cheeks, or temples |

*You can self-Haven or be Havened by another person.*

### Research Support

- **Ruden (2010):** Original clinical case series showed rapid resolution of PTSD and phobia symptoms
- **fMRI studies:** Havening reduces amygdala hyperactivity and increases prefrontal regulation
- **Christiansen et al. (2017):** Case studies demonstrating effectiveness for trauma and anxiety
- Havening is growing in clinical use among trauma therapists and EMDR practitioners

### Who Benefits

- Trauma and PTSD
- Phobias and panic attacks
- Intrusive memories and flashbacks
- Acute stress and overwhelm
- Sensitivity to criticism or rejection
- Performance anxiety

### What to Expect

Havening feels soothing — like being comforted. Sessions are shorter than traditional therapy (5–15 minutes for a specific trigger). Processing can feel gentle but profound. Some people report feeling more relaxed after a single session.
