# EFT Tapping Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

## Two-Minute Overview of Emotional Freedom Technique

---

### The Core Principle
Tapping on acupressure points sends calming signals to the amygdala, reducing the stress response while you focus on a problem — rewiring the brain's association with that trigger.

---

### The 9 Tapping Points

| Point | Abbrev. | Location |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Karate Chop** | KC | Side of hand, between pinky and wrist |
| **Eyebrow** | EB | Start of eyebrow, near bridge of nose |
| **Side of Eye** | SE | Bone at outer corner of eye |
| **Under Eye** | UE | Bone below the eye |
| **Under Nose** | UN | Between nose and upper lip |
| **Chin** | Ch | Crease between chin and lower lip |
| **Collarbone** | CB | Where collarbone meets sternum |
| **Under Arm** | UA | ~4 inches below armpit, side of body |
| **Top of Head** | TH | Crown of the head |

---

### The Basic Protocol (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Identify the Issue**
What's bothering you right now? Get specific.

**Step 2: Rate Intensity (0–10)**
0 = no disturbance; 10 = worst imaginable.

**Step 3: Setup Statement**
Tap KC continuously and repeat 3 times:
*"Even though I have this [problem/feeling], I deeply and completely accept myself."*

**Step 4: The Tapping Sequence**
Tap each point 5–7 times while repeating a reminder phrase (e.g., "this anxiety about the meeting," "this tightness in my chest").

**Order:** EB → SE → UE → UN → Ch → CB → UA → TH

**Step 5: Re-rate Intensity**
Has the number dropped? Repeat rounds until it reaches 0–2.

### When to Use EFT

| Situation | How to Adapt |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Anxiety attack** | Tap faster, focus on the physical sensation |
| **Phobia** | Tap while visualizing the feared situation from a distance |
| **Chronic pain** | Tap on the sensation itself — "this burning in my lower back" |
| **Insomnia** | Tap lying down; focus on "this racing mind" |
| **Craving** | Tap while thinking of the craved food/substance |
| **Performance** | Tap before a meeting, exam, or presentation |
| **Grief** | Slow, gentle tapping; allow tears |

---

### Common Setup Statements

- "Even though I feel [anxious/angry/scared/sad], I accept myself anyway."
- "Even though this situation feels impossible, I'm open to seeing it differently."
- "Even though I don't believe this tapping will work, I'll try it anyway."
- "Even though a part of me doesn't want to let this go, I honor that part."

---

### Signs of Release

- Deep sigh or yawn
- Sudden urge to take a deep breath
- Tears flowing
- Feeling heat or tingling
- The issue suddenly seems "farther away"
- Laughter or lightness

---

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Number won't drop | Check for a deeper layer or different aspect of the same issue |
| Can't focus | Tap on "this fogginess" or "this distraction" |
| Feeling worse | This is temporary — keep tapping or take a break and ground yourself |
| Unsure which point | All points are effective; even tapping a few is beneficial |
| No shift at all | Try a different setup statement or slower tapping |

---

### The 3-Minute Emergency Tapping Sequence

**Use when you need fast relief:**

1. **KC:** "Even though I'm overwhelmed right now, I'm safe and I'm okay." (3x)
2. **EB → SE → UE → UN → Ch → CB → UA → TH:** "This overwhelm…" (one round)
3. **Deep breath**
4. **EB → SE → UE → UN → Ch → CB → UA → TH:** "I'm choosing calm…" (one round)
5. **TH:** "Peace."

### Research Highlights

- Reduces cortisol by 24–43% in a single session (Church, 2010)
- Large effect sizes for anxiety, depression, and PTSD (Feinstein, 2019 — meta-analysis of 107 studies)
- 90% reduction in phobia symptoms in 4 sessions (Wells et al., 2003)
- fMRI evidence of reduced amygdala activation after tapping (Bach, 2019)
