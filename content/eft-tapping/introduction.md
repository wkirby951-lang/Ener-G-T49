# EFT Tapping — Emotional Freedom Technique

## What Is EFT Tapping?

EFT (Emotional Freedom Technique), often called "tapping," is a mind-body therapy that combines acupressure (stimulating meridian points on the body) with cognitive reframing (voicing affirmations or acknowledging a problem). It was developed in the 1990s by Gary Craig as a simplified form of Thought Field Therapy (TFT).

### How It Works

EFT is based on the premise that emotional distress is a disruption in the body's energy system. By tapping on specific acupressure endpoints while mentally focusing on a problem, you send a calming signal to the amygdala — the brain's fear center — and help rewire the stress response.

**The basic sequence:**
1. Identify the issue and rate its intensity (0–10)
2. Create a "setup statement" that acknowledges the problem while affirming self-acceptance
3. Tap through a standard sequence of meridian points while repeating a reminder phrase
4. Re-rate the intensity — repeat until it drops to 0 or manageable

### The Tapping Points

| # | Point | Location | What It Targets |
|---|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Karate Chop** (KC) | Side of hand, between pinky and wrist | Starting point for the setup statement |
| 2 | **Eyebrow** (EB) | Beginning of eyebrow, near the bridge of nose | General stress, fear |
| 3 | **Side of Eye** (SE) | On the bone at the outer corner of the eye | Anger, frustration |
| 4 | **Under Eye** (UE) | On the bone below the eye | Anxiety, worry |
| 5 | **Under Nose** (UN) | Between nose and upper lip | Embarrassment, shame |
| 6 | **Chin** (Ch) | Crease between chin and lower lip | Guilt, blame |
| 7 | **Collarbone** (CB) | Where collarbone meets sternum, just below the knot | Overwhelm, fear |
| 8 | **Under Arm** (UA) | About 4 inches below armpit, side of body | Social anxiety, sensitivity |
| 9 | **Top of Head** (TH) | Crown of the head | Center-ing, completion |

### Research Support

- **Church et al. (2010):** Randomized controlled trial showed EFT significantly reduced cortisol levels (the stress hormone)
- **Feinstein (2019):** Large-scale meta-analysis found EFT had large effect sizes for anxiety, depression, PTSD, and phobias — comparable to CBT
- **Bach et al. (2019):** fMRI study showed tapping reduced amygdala activation
- **Stapleton et al. (2020):** Clinical trial demonstrated EFT's effectiveness for food cravings and weight management

### Who Benefits

- Anxiety and stress (most common use)
- Phobias (flying, heights, spiders, public speaking)
- PTSD and trauma
- Chronic pain
- Insomnia and sleep issues
- Performance anxiety (sports, exams, public speaking)
- Food cravings and weight management

### What to Expect

Tapping is easy to learn and can be done anywhere — no equipment needed. Some people feel immediate relief, while others need a few rounds. It's common to experience a "yawn," a sigh, or a deep breath as the stress releases. EFT is gentle enough for daily self-care.
